New MIDAS

New Model Institute for Doctoral Academic Studies

Doctoral education in the UK is no longer fit for purpose. The viva voce exam­ination is highly problematic, while writing a thesis by hand in the age of large lang­uage models is anachron­istic. Doctoral projects and over-arching programs are not agile enough to adapt to real-world events that challenge their initial underpinning assump­tions. Student recruitment and educational processes discriminate against some cohorts whose potential contributions are therefore excluded.

Academia remains a bastion of coercive practices, under-payment, and excessive workloads. The wellbeing of students and super­visors – and the quality of their work – suffer as a result.

Innovative approaches are required in the education of research workers if high quality scientific research is to be sustainable.

A Critical Review of Doctoral Education in the United Kingdom

Introduction

Recognition that the number of researchers needs to increase: The United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals include “Enhance scientific research… encouraging innovation and substantially increasing the number of research and development workers per 1 million people” (United Nations 2024).

Context and rationale

Doctoral education in the UK

  • The PhD experience is well-known to be extremely stressful.
  • Doctoral-level education in the UK is not available to many who would make excellent candidates.
  • Manually writing a thesis – an entire book – in the age of AI and LLMs is an archaic approach.
  • The viva voce examination is highly problematic.
  • PhD projects – and over-arching doctoral programmes – are typically formulated in advance and hence cannot respond to the fast-moving real world.
