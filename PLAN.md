Start **lean and reversible**, then add entities only when needed for funding or risk. Here's a staged route with structures at each step.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 1 – Build software and establish the New MIDAS name

**Goal:** Ship early tools, publish academic papers under 'branded identity', keep costs and admin minimal.

- Start as **me + one company**:
	- A simple **My Lab Ltd** (standard private limited company).
	- Functions: IP ownership, software dev, first customers, light consulting.
	- Pros:
		- Fast to set up, cheap, minimal reporting.
		- Investors and customers understand it.
- Register **New MIDAS** as a dormant limited company, or (more probably) a dormant company limited by guarantee.
	- Cost ~£50 per annum
	- Allows me to refer to "the New MIDAS initiative" as something that is currently in development.
	- My research papers can list my affiliation as "My Lab Ltd" (no-one requires it to be a charity or university for that to appear) as part of "the New MIDAS initiatve".
	- Pros:
		- Legitimate, credible, cheap, name-protecting, and gives you a real company number to point to.
- When this phase ends:
	- I'll have some working software, a public website, and at least one or two papers or essays with "New MIDAS initiative" affiliation.
	- "My Lab Ltd" can reference its parent-in-progress: "My Lab Ltd (reg. no. XXXXXXX) is the founding entity of the New MIDAS initiative (reg. no. XXXXXXX)."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 2 – Add a research/think-tank layer

**Trigger:** I'm ready to publish more serious research and start applying for grants/donations; I want to be seen as an representing an independent research organisation.

Two main options here:

1. **Convert the brand into a charity and keep the Ltd as its trading arm**
	- Incorporate **My Charity** as CIO or charitable company.
	- The existing **My Lab Ltd** becomes its **wholly-owned trading subsidiary** (I transfer/shuffle shareholding).
	- Functions:
		- My Charity: research, publications, public interest work, future doctoral programme groundwork.
		- My Lab Ltd: software products and services, gifting profits to charity via Gift Aid.
	- This is a "classic think-tank stack" but reached gradually instead of built up-front.

2. **Keep the company as core and create a charitable research arm later**
	- Keep **My Lab Ltd** as the main vehicle and brand owner.
	- Later in Phase 2 (when ready for funders), set up **My Research Org** as a **separate charity**:
		- Charity: independent social-science research, policy work, ethics, critical theory.
		- Ltd: opinionated software and commercial work; has a formal partnership with the charity.
	- This leans toward the **"Activist Platform"** flavour from before: the company can be more overtly Gramscian; the charity remains clearly evidence-led and non-party-political.

If my main concern is *not scaring off progressive grant-making trusts*, option 1 is cleaner; if I care more about **unconstrained political tone** for the core brand, option 2 is better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 3 – Prepare for funding-driven growth

**Goal:** Become fundable as a credible research/think-tank actor and lay groundwork for doctoral disruption.

Typical moves:

- Charity (if I have one):
	- Start with **small research grants** and philanthropic funding rather than UKRI.
	- Build a **track record** of publications, events, networks with academics.
- Company:
	- Grow **ARR from institutional buyers**, so I can co-fund experiments and match-fund grants.
- Structural tweak (only if needed):
	- If I went with "charity + trading subsidiary", I can later **carve out** a separate "New Model Institute for Doctoral Academic Studies" ("New MIDAS") as a second charitable entity if the research/doctoral work becomes large and specialised.
	- If I went with "company + charity partner", I can later make the charity more prominent (new trustees, separate brand, more independent governance) to unlock bigger funders.

At this point I still have **max two entities**; a third only appears if the research/doctoral work gets large enough to justify it.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 4 – First steps toward a doctoral programme

**Trigger:** I have:
- A credible research track record.
- Some institutional respect and partners.
- Enough income (or committed grants) to support a small cohort.

Key moves (structural side):

- **Do not** try to get my own degree-awarding powers first; instead:
	- Use a **validation or partnership arrangement** with a university who awards the degrees while I provide most of the supervision/training.
	- Legally, the doctoral activity sits in the **charity** I've formed, or in **New MIDAS**.
- I can frame it as:
	- "New MIDAS Fellows" or similar, hosted by my charity/research body, registered for degrees at Partner University X.
- Structure likely at this point:
	- If I chose route 1:
	- "My Charity" + "My Lab Ltd" (trading).
	- If I chose route 2:
		- My Lab Ltd (core/brand) + My Research Org (charity partner).

I only add a **separate "Institute" entity** if I need another level of governance/ring-fencing (e.g., to pursue big UKRI/IRO status later, or to separate controversial political work from research).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Simple recommended path

Given that I'm starting solo and want to move fast:

1. **Now – Phase 1**
	- Form **My Lab Ltd**.
	- Build software, publish initial paper(s) with clear branded affiliation.

2. **When I have some traction – early Phase 2**
	- Decide between:
		- A. Turn "My Lab Ltd" into a **charity + trading subsidiary** and shift shares accordingly; *or*
		- B. Keep the company as core and create a **My Research Org** charity alongside it.
	- For maximum **flexibility of tone** and minimal immediate complexity, **B is usually easier**: add the charity later, when funders are in sight.

3. **Later – Phase 4**
	- Use the charity as the legal host for my first doctoral fellows, under a **validation arrangement** with a friendly university.
	- Consider a separate "Institute" or "Foundation" entity only after I've outgrown that two-entity setup.

The critical point: I do **not** need the full "three-entity" or "foundation + institute + lab" structure at the start. Begin with a **single Ltd**, add **one charity** when funding/funders make it worthwhile, and only then consider more elaborate structures if and when the work warrants it.
